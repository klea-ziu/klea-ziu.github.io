Machine learning for chemistry, catalysis, and sustainable discovery

I develop reliable and data-efficient ML methods for scientific discovery, including Neural ODEs for reaction kinetics, graph neural networks for catalyst design, and ML prediction of density of states (DOS).

My work sits at the intersection of machine learning and physical chemistry. I am especially interested in models that respect scientific structure while remaining practical for messy, limited, and expensive experimental data.

Research Focus

Methods for Scientific Discovery

  • Neural ODEs for reaction kinetics Learning dynamics that can model chemical reaction behavior with structure and interpretability.
  • Graph neural networks for catalyst design Representing materials and molecular structure for more efficient catalyst discovery.
  • ML prediction of density of states (DOS) Building models that connect electronic structure to useful downstream scientific decisions.
  • Data-driven sustainable chemistry Using ML to support clean energy, low-emission industrial processes, and scalable catalysis.
